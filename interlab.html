2016 InterLab Measurement Study

Background

For the past two years, iGEM has hosted the InterLab Measurement Study, utilising iGEM's resources and spirit of collaboration to create the two biggest interlaboratory studies ever done in synthetic biology. These studies seek to standardise the tools available to the synthetic biology community and focus on establishing a baseline for replicability of fluorescence measurements. This year, iGEM teams from around the world used two protocols to quantify the expression of five different reporter constructs. The measured fluorescence of Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP) was used as a proxy for promoter activity.

For this study, we used the following constructs:
J23101.B0034.E0040.B0015 as Test Device 1
J23106.B0034.E0040.B0015 as Test Device 2
J23117.B0034.E0040.B0015 as Test Device 3
I20270 as our Positive Control Device
R0040 as our Negative Control Device

All devices and parts were obtained from the iGEM 2016 InterLab Distribution Kit. Devices were made with a pSB1C3 plasmid backbone and produced in the E. coli strain NEB5-alpha.

Plate Reading Protocol

Calibration
LUDOX-S30 was used as a single point reference to obtain a conversion factor that transforms Abs600 absorbance data into a standard OD600 measurement. A 96-well plate was prepared with a column of 4 wells containing 100µl 100% LUDOX and 4 wells containing 100µl H2O. The absorbance 600nm of all samples in all standard measurement modes of our plate reader was measured, and our Reference OD600 data was divided by our Abs600 data to get our correction factor. Data was converted to OD600 measurements through multiplication by our correction factor.

200µl 2.5µM FITC stock solution was created and serially diluted 1:2 with 1x PBS across plate columns 1-11. 100µl of FITC 5x stock solution were mixed in with 100µl of PBS in each well. Column 12 contained only 100µl PBS buffer. This calibration plate was then measured in our plate reader under standard GFP settings and no path length correction to give us a standard measurement curve.
