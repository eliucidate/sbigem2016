Our Mission

The Stanford-Brown 2016 iGem team is building a biologically produced balloon for space exploration. Biological materials have a variety of advantages over traditional building materials, most importantly: they are light and they are self-replicating.

Both of these characteristics make functional biological components ideally suited for space exploration applications. To make a functional balloon, we need at least two elements. The balloon has to be made of something, and the balloon has to do something. To that end our team is working on both producing gas impermeable biological membranes, and creating biological sensors that can attach either to the membrane or be embedded in a balloon payload to detect specific small molecules in the atmosphere.
